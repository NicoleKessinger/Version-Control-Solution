Root Causes

Why This Keeps Happening

1

No Single Source of Truth

Files live across email, shared drives, local machines, and SaaS tools — with no authoritative record of which is current.

2

Manual Processes & Naming Conventions

Teams rely on file naming like "final_v2_REVISED_USE THIS ONE.docx" — a fragile, error-prone system that breaks under scale.

3

Siloed Teams & Tools

Legal, operations, and product each use different tools with no shared version layer — causing divergence and rework.

Compliance Risk
Without version audit trails, regulated industries face serious exposure during audits, reviews, and legal discovery.
AI Adoption Blocked
AI tools are only as reliable as the data they're trained on. Version chaos means AI outputs based on stale or conflicting source documents — undermining trust in automation.
Leadership Blind Spots
Executives can't make confident decisions when it's unclear which version of a policy, analysis, or report is current.

Core Capabilities

What the Platform Delivers

Single Source of Truth

Every document has one canonical home with full version history — no more hunting across drives, inboxes, or chat threads.

AI-Assisted Governance

AI surfaces conflicts, summarizes changes between versions, and flags documents at risk of becoming stale or out of compliance.

Audit-Ready Compliance

Immutable audit trails capture who changed what, when, and why — making compliance reviews fast and defensible.

Cross-Team Collaboration

Legal, ops, product, and finance work from the same version layer — eliminating silos and reducing rework.

Smart Notifications

Stakeholders are automatically notified when documents they own or depend on are updated, approved, or flagged.

Integrates with Your Stack

Native connectors for SharePoint, Google Drive, Confluence, Jira, Salesforce, and your existing SSO provider.

Branching & Parallel Drafts

Teams can work on competing drafts simultaneously without overwriting each other — then merge, compare, or promote the winning version with one click.

Role-Based Access Control

Granular permissions ensure the right people can view, edit, approve, or archive documents — with full visibility into who has access to what.
